## Variante A — Enterprise Advisory (Contrarian/Strategic Insight)

**Hook type:** Contrarian + pattern recognition
**Lógica:** Usa o voice marker "Most product teams ignore" como abertura e a descoberta do DORA Report de que AI é um espelho organizacional. Posiciona Marine como a estrategista que vê além do hype.

---

Most product teams ignore where AI-driven development actually fails.

It's not the technology. It's the organization behind it.

I've been tracking this trend across engineering orgs for the past year.

The 2025 DORA Report confirmed what I suspected: AI doesn't improve software delivery on its own. It amplifies whatever already exists. Strong teams get stronger. Fragmented teams get exposed.

80% of developers now use AI tools. Only 29% trust the output.

The data tells a different story than the one vendors are selling:

1. Only 6% of organizations have redesigned workflows around AI. The rest bolted tools onto broken processes.
2. Teams with high AI adoption merge 98% more pull requests, but review time jumped 91%.
3. Developers believe AI makes them 24% faster. Rigorous testing shows they're actually 19% slower.
4. 66% spend more time fixing "almost right" AI code than writing from scratch.

AI-driven development isn't a tool decision. It's an organizational design decision.

What's your org's biggest gap between AI adoption and actual delivery improvement?

## Variante B — Data-driven (Surprising Stat / Pipeline Problem)

**Hook type:** Surprising stat + pattern interrupt
**Lógica:** Abre com os números mais impactantes do Faros AI (98% mais PRs / 91% review time) e usa "The companies growing fastest are" como takeaway. Marine como quem conecta dados a decisão estratégica.

---

98% more pull requests. 91% longer review time.

AI-driven development has a pipeline problem nobody's talking about.

I've been tracking this trend in how engineering teams adopt AI coding tools.

The assumption is simple: AI writes code faster, so teams ship faster.

Faros AI studied 10,000+ developers across 1,255 teams. High-adoption teams completed 21% more tasks and merged 98% more PRs. But the human layer, code review, became the bottleneck. Review time increased 91%.

Here's where it gets worse:

1. Engineers merged 27% more PRs but worked 19.6% more out-of-hours. AI didn't save time. It redistributed it.
2. Experienced developers using AI took 19% longer on tasks. Yet they believed they were 24% faster.
3. Only 29% of developers trust AI output accuracy. 66% say they spend more time fixing near-correct code.
4. At the org level, there's no significant correlation between AI adoption and better delivery metrics.

The companies growing fastest aren't just adopting AI. They're redesigning their entire delivery pipeline around it.

Has your team experienced this bottleneck shift? Where did the constraint move to?
