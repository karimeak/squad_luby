Fintech compliance costs have tripled since 2020. Mid-sized players are disappearing. And most executives still aren't talking about it.

I've seen this story play out before. A well-funded fintech scales fast, builds a great product -- then hits a $5-15M annual compliance wall (SEC/OCC regulatory data, 2024). That's a 3-5x increase from just four years ago.

What executives miss is that this isn't just a cost problem. It's a consolidation engine. Venture funding dropped 40-50% from its 2021 peak of $238B down to $110-120B (CB Insights, McKinsey). When capital dries up and compliance costs spike, mid-market fintech gets squeezed from both sides.

Here's what the companies that win are doing differently:

1. Shifting to B2B -- B2B payments grew 18-22% CAGR vs. consumer fintech's 3-5% (McKinsey, Forrester)
2. Treating compliance as strategy, not overhead -- building it into the product from day one
3. Choosing vertical specialization over horizontal scale to reduce regulatory surface area
4. Partnering with well-capitalized players instead of competing against them
5. Making profitability the north star, not growth at all costs

The real question is not whether compliance costs will keep rising. They will. The question is whether your fintech strategy accounts for it before it becomes a crisis.
