78% of companies have adopted AI.
Most of them still can't tell you if it's making money.

Something I keep seeing with my clients: they invested in the tools, rolled out pilots, got the team excited — and then hit a wall.

Not a technology wall. A strategy wall.

The pattern is clear. Organizations are spending on AI, but 42% say they lack the skilled people to actually turn adoption into results (Netguru). Meanwhile, the ones who got it right are seeing $3.70 back for every dollar invested (Deloitte).

That's not a small gap. That's a canyon.

What most people overlook is that AI adoption and AI strategy are two completely different things. One is a purchase. The other is a transformation.

Here's what I tell every executive who asks me about this:

- Stop measuring AI by how many tools you deployed
- Start measuring it by revenue impact and decision speed
- Bring in strategic guidance before your third pilot, not after your tenth
- Treat AI consulting as an investment, not an expense

The companies winning with AI right now didn't just adopt it. They built a strategy around it — with the right people at the table.

If your AI spend is growing but your clarity isn't, that's the gap worth closing.

What's your experience — adoption without strategy, or strategy without execution?
