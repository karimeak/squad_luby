85% of enterprises will implement AI agents by end of 2025.

Only 39% can show any EBIT impact.

I've been tracking this trend closely. The data tells a different story than the hype. Companies are deploying agents everywhere — 57% already in production, 22% in pilot (Warmly, 2026). The market hit $7.6B and is growing at 45.8% CAGR (Grand View Research, 2025).

But here's what most product teams ignore: adoption and impact are completely disconnected.

Businesses report 55% higher operational efficiency with AI agents. They report 35% cost reductions. Yet most say the actual EBIT contribution is still below 5% (Appverticals, 2026).

The companies growing fastest are solving this gap:

1. They measure agents by revenue outcomes, not deployment count
2. They start with customer service (30% automated today, 50% by 2027)
3. They treat agents as product decisions, not IT projects
4. They accept that financial processes over 90% automated are the exception, not the baseline

$3.8B in VC funding poured into AI agent startups in 2024 alone — nearly 3x the previous year (Index.dev, 2025). Smart money sees the potential. Most product teams haven't figured out the execution.

Is your team measuring agent adoption or agent impact?
