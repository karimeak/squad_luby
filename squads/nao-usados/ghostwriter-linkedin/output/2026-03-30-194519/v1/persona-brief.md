---

## Ângulo Primário (para Variante A)

**Tipo:** Enterprise advisory / Strategic insight
**Ângulo:** "AI is a mirror, not a magic wand — what the DORA Report means for your product org"
**Justificativa:** Marine's audience (CPOs, Heads of Product) are the ones being asked to integrate AI into product workflows. The DORA "mirror and multiplier" finding is the perfect strategic framing — it says what Marine's audience needs to hear: AI amplifies your existing maturity, it doesn't replace strategy. Only ~6% of companies have redesigned workflows to capture AI's value. Marine can position herself as the strategist who sees past the hype to the organizational reality. This angle aligns perfectly with her tone (analytical, pattern-aware) and her objective (positioning as reference in B2B tech growth).

**Key data points:**
1. "AI is a mirror and multiplier of existing engineering maturity" — 2025 DORA Report (HIGH confidence)
2. "Only ~6% of organizations have fundamentally redesigned workflows to capture AI's value" — McKinsey (HIGH confidence)
3. "80% of developers use AI tools, but only 29% trust AI output accuracy" — Stack Overflow 2025 (HIGH confidence)

## Ângulo Alternativo (para Variante B)

**Tipo:** Data-driven / Pattern interrupt
**Ângulo:** "The 91% review bottleneck: Why your AI investment is making teams ship slower"
**Justificativa:** Marine can weaponize the Faros AI data to challenge the "AI = instant productivity" narrative that product leaders are being sold. The 98% more PRs + 91% longer review time is a systems-thinking story — Marine's strength. CPOs and Growth Leads need to understand that AI accelerates the wrong part of the pipeline if the delivery system isn't redesigned. This is exactly the kind of counterintuitive, data-backed insight that Marine's persona delivers.

**Key data points:**
1. "Teams with high AI adoption: 21% more tasks, 98% more PRs, but 91% longer review time" — Faros AI, 10,000+ devs (HIGH confidence)
2. "19.6% rise in out-of-hours commits" — suggesting AI redistributes work, doesn't reduce it (HIGH confidence)
3. "Experienced developers are 19% slower with AI tools — but believe they're 24% faster" — METR RCT (MEDIUM confidence)

## Dados Filtrados do Research

### USAR:
1. **DORA "mirror and multiplier"** — organizational maturity determines AI ROI. Marine's audience cares about org-level outcomes, not individual dev productivity. (Finding 4, HIGH confidence)
2. **91% review bottleneck + 98% more PRs** — concrete systems-level data that shows AI shifts the bottleneck, not removes it. Perfect for strategic framing. (Finding 1/7, HIGH confidence)
3. **29% trust + 66% fixing AI code** — the human layer problem. CPOs need to know their teams don't trust the tools they're investing in. (Finding 3, HIGH confidence)
4. **Only 6% redesigned workflows** — the gap between adoption and transformation. Marine can say "most orgs are doing it wrong." (Finding 4, HIGH confidence)
5. **METR: 19% slower, think they're 24% faster** — the perception gap is a brilliant strategic insight for product leaders making investment decisions. (Finding 2, MEDIUM confidence)

### DESCARTAR:
- **46% code AI-generated** (Finding 5) — vendor data, Marine should avoid low-confidence claims per her profile
- **322% more privilege escalation paths** (Finding 6) — security angle is for CTOs/engineers, not product leaders
- **Gartner 90% adoption by 2028** (Finding 8) — too generic, no strategic insight for Marine's audience

## Voice Markers Ativos

1. **"The data tells a different story"** — Perfect opener to challenge the AI hype narrative. Marine presents data that contradicts what vendors promise.
2. **"Most product teams ignore"** — Establishes authority by pointing to blind spots. "Most product teams ignore what happens after you hand AI-generated code to your review pipeline."
3. **"I've been tracking this trend"** — Positions Marine as someone who watches patterns over time, not reacting to headlines. "I've been tracking this trend across our clients' engineering orgs."
4. **"The companies growing fastest are"** — Closer/CTA marker. "The companies growing fastest are the ones redesigning their delivery pipeline around AI, not just adopting it."

---

## Evitar

- **Generic business advice** — "AI is changing everything" tipo de frase. Marine precisa de dados e padrões específicos.
- **Low-data claims** — Não usar nenhum dado marcado LOW confidence. Marine's credibility depends on verified data.
- **Overly casual tone** — Marine is analytical, not informal. No "wild, right?" or "let that sink in."
- **Technical jargon** — No "PR review pipelines" sem contexto. Marine's audience includes product leaders, not all are technical.
- **Developer-centric framing** — The story is about organizational strategy, not developer experience. Don't frame it as "developers are struggling with AI" — frame it as "organizations are investing in AI without a delivery strategy."
- **Vendor-sourced claims** — Marine's audience is skeptical of vendor data. Stick to DORA, METR, Stack Overflow — independent sources.
